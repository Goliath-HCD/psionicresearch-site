UAP Contact Protocols: The Science of Directed Consciousness and Non-Human Intelligence

Updated March 2026 · 9 min read · Research & Declassified

Among the least-discussed findings in the UAP (Unidentified Aerial Phenomena) research literature is a persistent pattern: the most credible close-encounter events appear to correlate with consciousness. Witnesses describe a sense of being perceived. Craft appear to respond to directed attention. Physical effects seem triggered by intentional mental states rather than random proximity.

This isn't fringe speculation. It's documented in government programs, peer-reviewed research, and the operational notes of some of the most credentialed UAP investigators in the field. And it places the science of anomalous cognition — specifically, controlled remote viewing — at the center of serious UAP contact research.

The Consciousness-UAP Connection in the Declassified Record

The Advanced Aerospace Weapons Systems Applications Program (AAWSAP), funded by the Defense Intelligence Agency beginning in 2007 and operated through BAASS (Bigelow Aerospace Advanced Space Studies), was the most comprehensive government investigation of UAP ever conducted. Its scope — prompted in part by cases like the 2004 USS Nimitz encounter — extended well beyond radar tracks and pilot testimony.

AAWSAP's research explicitly included research into anomalous cognition, poltergeist-like physical effects, and what the program termed "five observables" — characteristics of UAP that appeared to exceed known physics.

One of those observables was trans-medium travel: the ability of UAP to move seamlessly between air, space, and water. Another was low observability: the apparent ability to avoid detection when not being actively observed. And a third — less discussed in public forums — was what researchers described as apparent responsiveness to human attention and intention.

Multiple AAWSAP research reports, some now partially available through FOIA, documented encounters in which UAP behavior appeared to change based on the mental state of observers — appearing when researchers expected them, responding to directed thought, and demonstrating awareness of human consciousness that had no conventional explanation.

CE-5: Contact via Directed Consciousness

The CE-5 (Close Encounter of the Fifth Kind) protocol, developed by Dr. Steven Greer and the Center for the Study of Extraterrestrial Intelligence (CSETI), is the most structured civilian framework for attempting intentional contact with non-human intelligence. It is based on a specific premise: that UAP intelligences can perceive and respond to directed human consciousness.

CE-5 protocols typically involve:

  • Coherence building: Group meditation and consciousness-focusing exercises to produce a coherent, directed mental state
  • Mental beacon projection: Directed visualization of specific geometric forms, locations, or contact intentions
  • Light signaling: Coherent light (laser) directed at known or expected UAP locations as a physical correlate to mental contact attempts
  • Remote viewing integration: Some practitioners incorporate CRV-style targeting to attempt access to information about contact-relevant locations prior to field protocols
  • Documentation: Systematic recording of any anomalous responses — light phenomena, electromagnetic anomalies, witness experiences

The scientific validity of CE-5 contact claims remains contested. However, the methodological assumption — that trained human consciousness can serve as a communication vector — is no longer purely speculative. It is consistent with the anomalous cognition literature produced by the STARGATE program. And the methodology is already being tested in the field — most notably by Skywatcher's dogwhistle experiments, which combine psionic contact attempts with modern sensor arrays.

NIDS and the Skinwalker Ranch Data

The National Institute for Discovery Science (NIDS), founded by aerospace entrepreneur Robert Bigelow in 1995, conducted the most sustained scientific investigation of UAP and related phenomena ever attempted by a private entity. Its primary research site, the property in northeastern Utah later known as Skinwalker Ranch, was the subject of a multi-year, instrumented study by a team of credentialed scientists.

NIDS researchers documented hundreds of anomalous events at the property, including UAP sightings, cattle mutilations, poltergeist-type effects, and apparent structured intelligences. What distinguished their approach was methodological rigor: trained observers, instrumented measurements, and careful attention to researcher effects.

One of NIDS's most provocative findings — documented in Colm Kelleher and George Knapp's 2005 book Hunt for the Skinwalker — was that the phenomena appeared to be intelligence-directed and observer-aware. Anomalous events consistently occurred just outside the perimeter of direct observation or instrumentation. The phenomena seemed to know when they were being watched and adjusted accordingly.

This observation directly parallels findings from the PEAR laboratory's consciousness-machine interaction research at Princeton: that the act of directed observation by a trained human consciousness appears to influence physical systems in statistically significant ways. The underlying mechanism is unknown. The effect, across multiple independent research programs, is documented.
